Step by Step Guide to Crop Images Online

Everything you need to know about cropping images for free in your browser

What is an Image Cropper?

An image cropper is a tool that lets you remove unwanted areas from a photo or graphic by selecting the portion you want to keep. Our free online image cropper works entirely in your browser - no software to install, no files uploaded to a server, and no watermarks on the output.

Why Crop an Image?

There are many reasons to crop a photo online:

  • Remove Unwanted Backgrounds: Cut out distracting elements around the edges of your photo
  • Social Media Sizing: Crop to 1:1 square for Instagram, or 16:9 for YouTube thumbnails and cover photos
  • Focus on the Subject: Crop tightly around a person or object to improve composition
  • Profile Photos: Crop to a square or circle-ready format for avatars and profile pictures
  • Print Preparation: Crop to standard print aspect ratios like 4:3 or 3:2
Pro Tip: Use the 1:1 aspect ratio preset when cropping images for Instagram posts or profile photos. Use 16:9 for YouTube thumbnails, Facebook cover photos, and widescreen banners.

How to Crop an Image Online?

Follow these simple steps to crop your image using our free tool:

  • Upload your image using "Choose File" or drag and drop
  • The image appears in the crop editor - drag to draw your crop selection
  • Optionally select an aspect ratio preset (Free, 1:1, 16:9, 4:3, 3:2) or enter custom pixel dimensions
  • Drag the corner and edge handles to fine-tune your crop area
  • Choose your output format (JPG, PNG, or WebP)
  • Click Crop & Download to save your cropped image

Aspect Ratio Presets Explained

Free (Freehand)

Draw any crop selection with no constraints. The selection can be any shape - tall, wide, or square - making it perfect for custom compositions where you don't need a fixed ratio.

1:1 - Square Crop

The 1:1 square crop is the most popular aspect ratio for Instagram posts, profile photos, and product images. The crop selection is always a perfect square regardless of how you drag it.

16:9 - Widescreen

The 16:9 crop is the standard widescreen format used for YouTube thumbnails, Facebook and LinkedIn cover photos, and most video content. It's also the native aspect ratio of most modern screens.

4:3 - Standard

The 4:3 ratio is the classic standard for presentations, older photographs, and tablet displays. It's a balanced ratio suitable for general-purpose use.

3:2 - Photography Standard

The 3:2 ratio is the native aspect ratio of most DSLR and mirrorless cameras, and is used for standard 4x6 inch photo prints. Use this when preparing photos for professional printing.

Custom Pixel Size

Enter exact pixel dimensions to crop an image to a specific size. This is useful when a platform requires an exact image size - for example, 1200x628 pixels for Open Graph images.
