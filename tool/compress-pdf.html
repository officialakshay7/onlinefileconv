Everything you need to know about reducing PDF file size online

What is a PDF Compressor?

A PDF compressor is a tool that reduces the file size of your PDF document while preserving its content and visual quality as much as possible. Large PDFs can be difficult to email, upload, or share - our compress PDF online tool solves this instantly, with no software installation needed.

Why Compress a PDF?

There are many reasons to reduce PDF file size:

  • Email Attachments: Most email providers limit attachments to 10-25MB - compressing makes sharing easy
  • Faster Uploads: Smaller PDFs upload quicker to websites, portals, and cloud storage
  • Storage Savings: Reduce storage usage on your device or cloud drive
  • Web Performance: Smaller PDFs load faster when embedded in websites
  • Form Submissions: Many online forms have strict file size limits - compression helps you meet them
Pro Tip: The Medium compression level is the best starting point for most PDFs - it offers a good balance between file size reduction and visual quality. Use High only when you need the smallest possible file and quality is less critical.

Understanding Compression Levels

Low Compression - Best Quality

The Low compression level applies minimal compression, stripping unused metadata and optimising the PDF structure while keeping all images at near-original quality. Best used when you need a slightly smaller file without any noticeable quality change.

Medium Compression - Recommended

The Medium compression level recompresses embedded images at a reduced quality and removes non-essential data. This is the recommended setting for most users - it typically reduces file size by 30-60% while keeping documents looking sharp on screen and in print.

High Compression - Smallest File Size

The High compression level applies aggressive image recompression to achieve the smallest possible file size. This is ideal when you need to compress PDF to 100KB or meet a very tight size requirement. Some reduction in image sharpness may be visible at this level.

Did You Know? Our free PDF compressor runs entirely in your browser using PDF.js and PDF-lib - your PDF is never uploaded to any server. All processing happens locally on your device, keeping your documents completely private and secure.

How Much Can I Reduce a PDF File Size?

The amount of compression depends on your PDF's content:

  • Image-heavy PDFs (scanned documents, photo books): Can be reduced by 50-80% with High compression
  • Mixed PDFs (text + images): Typically reduced by 30-60% with Medium compression
  • Text-only PDFs: Typically reduced by 10-30% since there are no images to compress

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I reduce a PDF file size?

The reduction depends on the content of your PDF. PDFs with many high-resolution images can be reduced by 50-80%. Text-only PDFs may see smaller reductions of 10-30%.

Will compressing a PDF reduce its quality?

Using the Low or Medium compression level preserves visual quality very well and is suitable for professional use. The High compression level reduces file size the most but may slightly reduce image sharpness in PDFs with many photos or graphics.

Is my PDF uploaded to a server?

No. Our compress PDF online free tool runs entirely in your browser. Your PDF is never sent to any server - all processing happens locally on your device, keeping your document completely private.

Can I compress a PDF to a specific size like 100KB or 1MB?

Our tool offers Low, Medium, and High compression levels. For very specific target sizes, try different levels and see which gets closest to your target. For very large PDFs with heavy images, the High setting gets the closest to compressing a PDF to 100KB.
