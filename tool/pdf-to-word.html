Step by Step Guide to Convert PDF to Word

Everything you need to know about converting PDF documents into editable Word files

What is a PDF to Word Converter?

A PDF to Word converter is a tool that extracts the text content of a PDF document and places it into an editable Microsoft Word DOCX file. Our free online PDF to Word tool reads the text layer of your PDF using PDF.js — entirely in your browser, with no files ever sent to a server.

Why Convert PDF to Word?

There are many common reasons to convert a PDF to Word:

  • Edit Content: PDFs are not easily editable — converting to Word lets you update text, fix errors, or restructure content freely
  • Reuse Text: Extract contract text, article content, or report data to reuse in other documents without retyping
  • Collaboration: Share an editable Word document with colleagues who need to add comments or make changes using track changes
  • Template Creation: Convert a PDF template into a reusable Word document that others can fill in
  • Translation: Paste extracted Word content into translation tools for easier multilingual conversion
Pro Tip: Our tool works best with digitally created PDFs that contain a real text layer — such as exported reports, contracts, articles, and invoices. If you can click and highlight text in your PDF using a PDF viewer, this tool will extract it successfully.

How to Convert PDF to Word Online?

  • Click Extract & Convert to process all pages
  • Review the extracted content in the live Word-style document preview

How the PDF to Word Conversion Works

Text Extraction with PDF.js

Our tool uses PDF.js to read the text content layer of each page in your PDF. Every text element on the page — including its content, position, and size — is read and used to reconstruct paragraphs in reading order, preserving the logical flow of the document.

Paragraph Reconstruction

Text items are grouped into lines by comparing their vertical positions, and lines are grouped into paragraphs by detecting vertical gaps. This preserves the natural paragraph structure of the original document in the Word output, making the extracted content much easier to read and edit than a raw text dump.

Multi-Page Support with Page Sections

For multi-page PDFs, each page is extracted as a clearly labelled section in the Word document. A page break is inserted between sections, and each section starts with a page label so you can easily identify where content came from in the original PDF.

Did You Know? Our PDF to Word converter free tool builds the DOCX file from scratch using the Open XML standard — entirely in your browser, with zero external libraries beyond PDF.js. Your PDF is never sent to any server, keeping your document completely private.

What PDFs Work Best?

  • Best results: Digitally created PDFs — exported Word documents, reports, contracts, articles, and invoices that contain a real text layer
  • Good results: PDFs with consistent paragraph structure and clear reading order
  • Limited results: PDFs with complex multi-column layouts, heavy use of tables, or decorative text elements
  • Not supported: Scanned PDFs (image-only) — these require OCR technology which cannot run in a browser-only environment

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert a multi-page PDF to Word?

Yes. Our tool extracts text from all pages of your PDF and organises them as clearly labelled sections in a single Word document, with a page break between each page's content.

What kind of PDFs work best?

PDFs with selectable text work best — such as digitally created PDFs, exported reports, contracts, and articles. If you can highlight text in your PDF using a PDF viewer like Adobe Reader or your browser, our tool will be able to extract it.

Is my PDF uploaded to a server?

No. Our PDF to Word online tool runs entirely in your browser using PDF.js. Your PDF is never sent to any server — all processing happens locally on your device, keeping your document completely private and secure.

Will the Word document keep the same formatting as the PDF?

Our tool preserves the text content and paragraph structure of your PDF. However, some complex formatting — such as multi-column layouts, custom fonts, and decorative elements — may not transfer perfectly, as browser-based text extraction cannot fully replicate the visual layout of a PDF.
